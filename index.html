A Tale of Two Markets: Public vs. Private

Decade-long forecasts reveal a stark divergence. Public markets are expected to deliver modest, single-digit returns, while private markets are poised to capture a significant illiquidity premium.

Public Market Forecasts (Annualized)

Public equities and bonds are projected to offer limited growth, making it challenging to meet high return targets through traditional allocations alone.

Private Market Forecasts (Annualized)

Alternatives are expected to significantly outperform, driven by structural advantages, the AI tailwind, and less efficient markets.

The Endowment Model: A Blueprint for Success

Top university endowments have consistently generated ~9%+ returns by embracing a long-term, equity-biased strategy with a heavy concentration in illiquid alternative assets.

Typical Ivy League Allocation

A typical allocation for a leading endowment like Harvard demonstrates a radical departure from the traditional 60/40 portfolio. Over two-thirds of the portfolio is committed to private equity and hedge funds, aggressively pursuing higher returns by harvesting the illiquidity premium.

  • Private Equity & Venture Capital: The primary growth engine.
  • Hedge Funds: For diversification and absolute returns.
  • Public Equities & Real Assets: For liquidity and inflation hedging.
  • Bonds & Cash: Primarily for liquidity management.
